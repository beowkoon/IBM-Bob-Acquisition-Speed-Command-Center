IBM Bob Acquisition Speed Command Center — Knowledge Library

=== DAY 1 READINESS ===
Day 1 readiness requires completion of critical accounting, finance, payroll, legal, and access control activities.
All employees must have systems access confirmed before Day 1.
Payroll continuity is a non-negotiable Day 1 requirement — any gap must be escalated immediately.
Legal entity structure must be confirmed and communicated to all functional leads before Day 1.
A Day 1 readiness review meeting should be held 2 weeks before the target date with all workstream owners.

=== LEGAL ENTITY SIMPLIFICATION ===
Legal entity simplification should be reviewed jointly by Legal, Tax, Treasury, and business leadership.
Entities with no employees, no revenue, and no active contracts are strong dissolution candidates.
Entities with duplicate IBM presence and no regulatory requirement are strong merger candidates.
Regulatory-required entities must be retained regardless of size or activity.
All simplification decisions require Legal sign-off at minimum; dissolution and merger require Tax and Treasury.
Target entity simplification completion by Day 100 to reduce ongoing admin and audit costs.

=== FINANCE AND ACCOUNTING ===
Chart of accounts mapping must be completed before the first consolidated close.
Use prior acquisition playbooks to identify standard mapping, governance, and SME routing steps.
Finance mapping should identify all cost centers, profit centers, and intercompany relationships.
Tax registration gaps must be resolved before any revenue is recognized in the acquired entity.
Budget variances exceeding 10% of plan should be escalated to the Integration Lead immediately.

=== WORKFORCE AND HR ===
Workforce alignment should confirm all reporting lines, role changes, and affected employees before Day 100.
HR data migration must include headcount, compensation, benefits, and employment contract details.
All employees should receive integration communications within the first week after close.
Change management planning should begin at close and run through to Day 100 stabilization.

=== RISK AND CONTROLS ===
All Critical and High risks must have an assigned owner and mitigation plan within 48 hours of identification.
Risk register should be reviewed weekly by the Integration Lead and updated after each major milestone.
Unmitigated risks linked to Day 1 activities should be escalated to the executive sponsor immediately.
Controls should be assessed for all acquired entities to identify gaps versus IBM standards.

=== SME ROUTING ===
Use the SME Directory to identify the correct functional owner for each integration question.
If the primary SME is unavailable, engage the backup SME before escalating to the escalation path.
SME coverage gaps should be flagged to the Integration Lead for reassignment within 24 hours.
Regional SMEs should be engaged for all country-specific tax, legal, and payroll questions.

=== LESSONS LEARNED ===
Early engagement of Tax and Legal avoids last-minute blockers to Day 1 readiness.
Incomplete data uploads are the most common cause of dashboard metric inaccuracies — validate all files at upload.
Legal entity simplification decisions made early in the integration cycle yield the highest cash release benefit.
Dashboard metrics should be refreshed after each major data upload or workstream status change.
Dedicated integration leads for each functional workstream significantly accelerate Day 100 outcomes.
